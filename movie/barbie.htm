Barbie review

*Barbie* takes us into the vibrant and flawless world of Barbieland, where everything is perfect until the titular Barbie (Margot Robbie) begins to question her existence. Guided by another Barbie (Kate McKinnon), she embarks on a journey to the real world, with Ken (Ryan Gosling) tagging along, causing chaos. The film’s stunning production design and pastel pink world are a visual treat, with strong performances from Robbie, Gosling, and especially America Ferrera, who delivers a powerful monologue on women’s equality. The script, written by Greta Gerwig and Noah Baumbach, balances feminist themes with sharp humor and musical numbers, delivering a satirical yet entertaining story. While the film’s message is deliberate and heavy-handed, Gerwig’s direction turns *Barbie* into a thought-provoking, risk-taking piece that transcends being just a corporate cash grab and positions itself as a contender for major awards.
