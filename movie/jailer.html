jailer review

*Jailer* follows retired jailer Muthuvel (Rajinikanth), now a family man, whose son, a police officer, goes missing and is presumed dead while investigating idol thieves. Devastated, Muthuvel sets out to avenge his son’s death. The film, directed by Nelson, takes its time to build momentum, with a slow start before picking up in the second half with mass action scenes. The dark comedy works well for fans of Nelson's style, but may not translate as effectively across languages. The film features cameos from stars like Shivarajkumar and Mohanlal, adding star power, while Rajinikanth delivers his signature style, though the emotional core of the father-son bond is underdeveloped. Despite a few solid performances, the film suffers from a dragged climax and lacks depth in its emotional moments. Anirudh’s background score enhances the action, but overall, *Jailer* struggles to live up to expectations.
