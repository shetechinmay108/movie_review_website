rrr review

*Ceasefire*, directed by Prashanth Neel, is an action-packed political drama set in the dystopian city of Khansar, spanning from 1747 to the present. The film follows Deva (Prabhas) and Vardharaja (Prithviraj Sukumaran) as they navigate power struggles, loyalty, and betrayal. With a focus on drama and action, it explores complex themes of leadership and political machinations. Prabhas delivers a compelling performance as Deva, balancing raw aggression with emotional depth, while Prithviraj adds complexity as Vardharaja. The film’s slow-burn first half sets up the characters and world, with a dark color palette and unique musical choices that enhance the drama. The cinematography and Ravi Basrur's soundtrack complement the film’s intense atmosphere, though the pacing could feel slow for some. The violence may not appeal to all audiences, but for those drawn to grand narratives, *Ceasefire* offers a gripping tale of power and brotherhood, with standout performances from the lead actors and strong supporting cast. Fans of intense, epic storytelling will find much to appreciate in this film, though patience is required in the initial stages.
