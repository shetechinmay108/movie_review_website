John Wick review

*John Wick: Chapter 4* continues the franchise's legacy of thrilling action, offering high-octane fight choreography and close-quarter combat with Keanu Reeves at his best. The film expands the mythos of the High Table and introduces new memorable characters, such as Scott Adkins as the over-the-top villain Killa and Donnie Yen as the skilled Caine. Martial arts legends like Hiroyuki Sanada and Marko Zaror also deliver standout performances. Bill Skarsgård plays the menacing Marquis, while Shamier Anderson and his dog bring an interesting dynamic. Rina Sawayama shines with her own action sequences. The film shines with its meticulously choreographed fight scenes, shot in wide frames without quick cuts, making it feel like a live-action video game. Director Chad Stahelski and Reeves highlight the importance of stunt work, and *JW4* sets a new gold standard for action cinema, surpassing the spectacle of the previous films.
