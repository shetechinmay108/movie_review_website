John Wick review

*Joker* tells the haunting story of Arthur Fleck (Joaquin Phoenix), a struggling clown and aspiring comedian living in a deteriorating Gotham City. The film, directed by Todd Phillips, dives deep into Arthur’s unraveling mental state as he faces a harsh, unforgiving world. Rather than glorifying mental illness, the film explores his descent into madness as he creates his infamous alter ego, the Joker. Set in the gritty 1980s, Gotham serves as a backdrop for the dark tale, with a soundtrack that amplifies the unease. Joaquin Phoenix delivers an unforgettable performance, fully embodying Arthur’s transformation from a broken man into a chilling villain. His portrayal, which is both disturbing and captivating, stands out as one of the most intense and immersive performances in recent memory. The film draws comparisons to *Taxi Driver* with its themes of alienation and descent into violence, and Phoenix’s portrayal of the Joker may well become the definitive origin story of the character. It's a disturbing yet brilliant film, likely deserving of major accolades, particularly for Phoenix’s performance.
