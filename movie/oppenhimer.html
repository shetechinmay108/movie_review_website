Oppenheimer review

Christopher Nolan's *Oppenheimer* is a haunting exploration of love, regret, and human disillusionment. The film, based on the biography *American Prometheus*, delves into the life of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the physicist behind the atomic bomb, and his ultimate downfall. The nonlinear narrative captures key events from his life, including his political activism, suspicions of espionage, and the moral consequences of his discoveries. Nolan’s direction, combined with powerful performances from Cillian Murphy, Robert Downey Jr., and others, creates a gripping and emotionally charged experience. The film's striking visuals, unsettling sound design, and Ludwig Göransson’s score intensify the anxiety as Oppenheimer grapples with guilt and the irreversible impact of his actions. *Oppenheimer* is a masterful portrayal of a man torn apart by his own brilliance and its consequences.
